Defining design

Design to me is the amalgmation of Art and Functionality. Art may or may not serve a purpose. Design however, is more specific than art. Any functioning element needs to be presented with some artistic approach, which according to me is the most apt representation of design. Here is an example which may help you understand Design from my perspective.

A keyboard can be designed in many ways. With Functionality as a priority, a keyboard may look like something on the lefttop with everything in perfect order and it would work. With much more artistic freedom, it can also look like something on the rightbottom and it would work as well.
A logical keyboard is something you see on your keyboard. Design is studying frequency of the keys used and placing them together. It is assigning more than one value to some keys and having a long space bar key to improve functioning.

Photo manipulation - Case Study

click on the image below to see the Sergio Ramos in a Barcelona Jersey Wallpaper

photo manipulation

There are multiple ways to manipulate photos like the one done above. One way is to change to look of the kit or the shirt in the photo using the same player in the photo. I have used the same trick in this photo. A simple selection of the tshirt area and changing the color, contrast, hue and saturation to match the one of the tshirt you want. The next step is to delete the logo, sponsor signs and any stripes or textures from the photo. Using the logo and sponsor vectors of the new club, attach the same on the existing photo, and there you have it a player who has chanegd his team.

An another and a bit tricky way to do this is using 2 different photos and combining them into one. This technique is useful when you try to swap jerseys with multiple varying textures. For example here, it would be tremendously difficult to get bothe these yellow and red colors to combine into the exact white shape. So instead, using a photo where the player is wearing the required kirt and is in the similat stance, select the tshirt from the new photo, apply the tshirt layer on the photo you want to edit and warp the tshirt onto the new player. This process is a bit tedious but it gives better results while trying to swap jerseys with a lot of texture difference.

Architect's Chess - A Case Study

I see the world with a polarized perspective, just like a Chess Board. It is all white or all black for me. I am also fascinated by architecture all around the world. So, I created a chess set inspired my modern architecture.

The basis of the chess pieces are manipulated versions of basic trigonometric shapes the triangle, the square and the circle.

I wanted to exploit the basic modeling tools on the software as much as possible. I wanted to create something using a combination of sculpting tools which followed a similar pattern. Hence, a chess set. The inspiration being Architerctural Building Shapes and Monument Design. Starting with the design of the pawn, I went on to create a structure combining squares and creating pillars. It took me around 1 week to finally build a concept and a design for the pawn. There it was ready, the first piece. Being a set, it needed a to follow a certain pattern. The basic shapes of concepts of Trigonometry, the triangle, the square, a circle were the basis for creating each of the Knight, Rook and the Bishop. Now, the King and the Queen, were simple yet tall standing structures, once again with the same bases of Square and Circle, the King standing tall and the elegant Queen, with the detailed curve climbing up the structure.
